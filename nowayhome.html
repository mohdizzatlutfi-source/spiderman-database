SPIDER-MAN: NO WAY HOME (2021)

Spider-Man: No Way Home is a 2021 American superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Spider-Man. Produced by Columbia Pictures, Marvel Studios, and Pascal Pictures, and distributed by Sony Pictures Releasing, it is the sequel to Spider-Man: Homecoming (2017) and Spider-Man: Far From Home (2019), and the 27th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). The film was directed by Jon Watts, written by Chris McKenna and Erik Sommers, and stars Tom Holland as Peter Parker / Spider-Man alongside Zendaya, Benedict Cumberbatch, Jacob Batalon, Jon Favreau, Jamie Foxx, Willem Dafoe, Alfred Molina, Benedict Wong, Tony Revolori, Marisa Tomei, Andrew Garfield, and Tobey Maguire. In the film, Peter asks Dr. Stephen Strange (Cumberbatch) to use magic to make his identity as Spider-Man a secret again after it was revealed to the world at the end of Far From Home. When the spell goes wrong due to Peter's actions, the multiverse is broken open and several visitors from alternate realities are brought into Peter's universe.

A third MCU Spider-Man film was planned during the production of Homecoming in 2017. Negotiations between Sony and Marvel Studios to alter their deal—in which they produce the Spider-Man films together—ended with Marvel Studios leaving the project in August 2019, but a negative fan reaction led to a new deal between the companies a month later. Watts, McKenna, Sommers, and Holland were set to return, and filming took place from October 2020 to March 2021 in New York City and Atlanta. No Way Home serves as a crossover between the MCU and the previous Spider-Man films directed by Sam Raimi and Marc Webb. Several actors reprise their roles from those films, including previous Spider-Man actors Maguire and Garfield. The pair's involvement was the subject of wide speculation and numerous leaks despite Sony, Marvel, and the cast's efforts to conceal their involvement until the film's release.

Spider-Man: No Way Home premiered at the Fox Village Theater in Westwood, Los Angeles, on December 13, 2021, and was theatrically released in the United States on December 17, as part of Phase Four of the MCU. The film received positive reviews from critics and grossed $1.921 billion worldwide, surpassing its predecessor as the highest-grossing film released by Sony Pictures. It became the highest-grossing film of 2021, the sixth-highest-grossing film at the time of its release, the highest-grossing Spider-Man film, and set several other box office records, including those for films released during the COVID-19 pandemic. The film received a nomination for Best Visual Effects at the 94th Academy Awards, among numerous other accolades. An extended version of the film, subtitled The More Fun Stuff Version, had a global theatrical release in September 2022. An additional film trilogy with Holland is planned, beginning with Spider-Man: Brand New Day, which is scheduled for release in July 2026.

PLOT

After Quentin Beck frames Peter Parker for his murder and reveals that Peter is Spider-Man,[a] the Department of Damage Control arrests Peter; his girlfriend, Michelle "MJ" Jones-Watson; his best friend, Ned Leeds; and his aunt, May Parker. Lawyer Matt Murdock gets Peter's charges dropped, but the group grapples with negative publicity. After Peter's, MJ's, and Ned's MIT applications are rejected, Peter goes to the New York Sanctum to ask Dr. Stephen Strange for help. Strange starts casting a spell that would make everyone forget Peter is Spider-Man, but it is corrupted when Peter repeatedly requests alterations to let his loved ones retain their memories. Strange contains the corrupted spell.

At Strange's suggestion, Peter tries to convince an MIT administrator to reconsider MJ's and Ned's applications. He is attacked by Otto Octavius, who rips nanotechnology from Peter's Iron Spider suit. This bonds with Octavius's mechanical tentacles and allows Peter to control them. As Norman Osborn arrives and attacks, Strange teleports Peter back to the Sanctum and locks Octavius in a cell next to Curt Connors. Strange explains that the corrupted spell summoned people from other universes within the multiverse who know Spider-Man's identity. He orders Peter, MJ, and Ned to find and capture the others; they locate and retrieve Max Dillon and Flint Marko at a military research facility.

Osborn reclaims control of himself from his split Green Goblin personality and destroys the Goblin mask. He gets help from May until Peter retrieves him. While discussing their battles with Spider-Man, Osborn, Octavius, and Dillon realize they were pulled from their universes just before their deaths.[b] Strange prepares to reverse the contained spell and send the villains back to their respective universes, but Peter argues that they should first help each villain to possibly change their fates upon their return. When Strange refuses, Peter steals the spell, traps Strange in the Mirror Dimension, and takes the villains to Happy Hogan's apartment. He uses Stark Industries technology to cure Octavius. Before Peter can cure anyone else, the Goblin persona retakes control of Osborn. The Goblin convinces the other villains to betray Peter and fatally wounds May as Dillon, Marko, and Connors escape; before she dies, May tells Peter that "with great power, there must also come great responsibility".

Ned discovers that he can create portals using Strange's sling ring, which he and MJ use to try to locate Peter. They instead find "Peter-Two" and "Peter-Three", alternate versions of Peter who were also summoned from the villains' universes by Strange's spell. The group finds this universe's Peter ("Peter-One"), who is mourning May and ready to send the villains home to die. The alternate Peters share stories of losing Uncle Ben[c] and Gwen Stacy[d] and encourage Peter-One to fight in May's honor. The three Peters develop cures for the villains and lure them to the Statue of Liberty.

Peter-One and Peter-Two cure Connors and Marko while Octavius helps cure Dillon. Ned accidentally frees Strange from the Mirror Dimension. The Goblin unleashes the contained spell, breaking the barriers between universes and pulling in countless others who know Peter's identity. Strange attempts to hold them off while an enraged Peter-One tries to kill the Goblin. Peter-Two stops him and Peter-Three helps Peter-One inject the Goblin with his cure, restoring Osborn's sanity.

Peter-One realizes that the only way to protect the multiverse is to erase himself from everyone's memory and requests Strange do so while promising MJ and Ned that he will find them and remind them who he is. Strange reluctantly casts the spell, and everyone returns to their respective universes—including Eddie Brock,[e] who unknowingly leaves behind a piece of the Venom symbiote. Two weeks later, Peter visits MJ to reintroduce himself to her and Ned, but decides against it. While mourning at May's grave, he has a conversation with an unknowing Hogan and is inspired to carry on, making a new Spider-Man suit and resuming his vigilantism.

PRODUCTION

DEVELOPMENT

During production on Spider-Man: Homecoming (2017), two sequels were being planned by Marvel Studios and Sony Pictures.[52] In June 2017, star Tom Holland explained that each film would take place during a different year of high school for Peter Parker / Spider-Man, with the third being set during the character's senior year.[53] Marvel Studios President Kevin Feige noted in July 2019 that the third film would feature "a Peter Parker story that has never been done before on film" due to the ending of Spider-Man: Far From Home (2019), which publicly reveals that Peter is Spider-Man.[54]

Development on third and fourth MCU Spider-Man films had begun by August 2019, with Sony hoping Holland and director Jon Watts would return for both.[55] However, contract discussions between Disney and Sony broke down regarding profit splits, leading Sony to announce moving forward on the franchise without Marvel Studios or Feige.[55][58] Following immense fan backlash and personal intervention from Holland with Disney CEO Bob Iger and Sony chairman Tom Rothman,[60] a new deal was reached in September 2019 allowing Marvel Studios and Feige to produce a third film for Sony with Amy Pascal.[4] Disney co-financed 25% of the budget in exchange for 25% of the profits while retaining merchandising rights.[4][61] Watts entered final negotiations to return as director.[62]

In October 2019, Iger credited the new deal to Holland and fan support,[63] while Rothman described the agreement as a "win-win-win for Sony, Disney, and the fans".[64] Zendaya was confirmed to reprise her role as MJ shortly after.[8][9]

PRE-PRODUCTION

Screenwriters Chris McKenna and Erik Sommers began writing in earnest by December 2019.[30][15] They initially considered Kraven the Hunter as the main villain,[30] but transitioned the plot to an It's a Wonderful Life (1946)-inspired story featuring Dr. Stephen Strange and the multiverse.[30] Once they committed to bringing back past Spider-Man characters, they wrote for all the returning actors optimistically.[30] Norman Osborn / Green Goblin was subsequently made the main villain to give him a darker, second-chance arc.[68] America Chavez was also considered at one point before her debut was shifted to Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness (2022).[69]

Production faced delays due to the COVID-19 pandemic, shifting release dates and requiring story revisions as No Way Home was now set to release before Multiverse of Madness.[71][72] In June 2020, Marisa Tomei confirmed her return as Aunt May,[29] and Charlie Cox was contacted by Feige to reprise his role as Matt Murdock / Daredevil.[73] By October 2020, Jacob Batalon, Benedict Cumberbatch, and Jamie Foxx (returning as Electro from The Amazing Spider-Man 2) were set to star.[16][11]

FILMING

Second unit filming began in New York City from October 14–16, 2020, under the working title Serenity Now,[78][81] capturing visual effects plates in Queens and Greenwich Village.[80][83] Principal photography moved to Atlanta on October 25 at Trilith Studios, with Mauro Fiore serving as cinematographer.[84][88] Strict safety protocols, digital scanning, and special light systems were implemented on set due to the COVID-19 pandemic.[93][94]

By December 2020, Alfred Molina (Doctor Octopus), Tobey Maguire, and Andrew Garfield joined the production.[24][30] Returning actors were brought to set wrapped in cloaks to prevent leaks.[106] McKenna and Sommers rewrote the introductions for Maguire and Garfield along with much of the third act right around Christmas 2020 as filming progressed.[15] Filming wrapped on March 26, 2021.[115]

POST-PRODUCTION

Post-production saw confirmation of returning cast members including Willem Dafoe, Thomas Haden Church, and Rhys Ifans.[21] Tom Hardy filmed a mid-credits cameo as Eddie Brock / Venom directed by Jon Watts.[41][15] Visual effects were created by vendors including Sony Pictures Imageworks, Framestore, Digital Domain, and Cinesite.[143] De-aging technology was utilized for Molina and Dafoe to preserve their appearances from previous franchises.[23][25]

MUSIC

Michael Giacchino returned to compose the score for No Way Home, which was released digitally on December 17, 2021.[148][150] The score thoughtfully incorporates classic musical themes composed by Danny Elfman, James Horner, Christopher Young, and Hans Zimmer from prior Spider-Man film eras, alongside Giacchino's own theme from Doctor Strange (2016).[151][152]

MARKETING & RELEASE

MARKETING

In May 2020, Sony entered a promotional partnership with Hyundai Motor Group to showcase their new models in the film, leading to a commercial directed by Watts starring Holland and Batalon.[154][155] In late February 2021, Holland, Batalon, and Zendaya released three fake titles—Phone Home, Home-Wrecker, and Home Slice—to tease fans before officially announcing the title, Spider-Man: No Way Home, in a viral whiteboard video.[113][156] In July 2021, Marvel revealed various figurines, Funko Pops, Marvel Legends figures, and Lego sets for the film.[160]

An initial leak of the teaser trailer circulated on social media on August 22, 2021, which was quickly taken down by Sony.[163] The teaser trailer officially premiered at CinemaCon 2021 on August 23.[19] It set a new global record with 355.5 million views in its first 24 hours—surpassing Avengers: Endgame (289 million)—and generated 4.5 million social media mentions globally.[172] The second trailer premiered at a fan screening on November 16, 2021,[173] stirring intense online speculation over whether Maguire and Garfield had been digitally edited out of shots like the Lizard being hit by an invisible force.[176][177]

Promotional partnerships included Fortnite, PUBG Mobile, Asus, Xiaomi, iQOO, Continental AG, and Tampico Beverages, generating a total promotional marketing value of $202 million.[181] Sony also launched viral The Daily Bugle videos on TikTok featuring J.K. Simmons and Angourie Rice, as well as an authentic Daily Bugle newsstand setup in New York City in partnership with Liberty Mutual.[178][180]

THEATRICAL RELEASE

Spider-Man: No Way Home held its world premiere at the Fox Village Theater in Westwood, Los Angeles, on December 13, 2021.[182][183] It was released in the United Kingdom and Ireland on December 15,[184] and in the United States on December 17, opening in 4,325 theaters in RealD 3D, IMAX, and ScreenX formats.[185][186][188] It is part of Phase Four of the MCU.[187] The film was re-released in theaters on June 3, 2024, as part of Columbia Pictures' 100th anniversary celebration.[193]

THE MORE FUN STUFF VERSION

An extended cut titled The More Fun Stuff Version was announced in June 2022 to celebrate 60 years of Spider-Man in comics and 20 years in film.[197] Releasing theatrically on August 31, 2022 internationally and September 1 in the US and Canada, it added approximately 13 minutes of new and deleted scenes.[198][199] Additions included an introduction by Holland, Maguire, and Garfield, expanded DODC interrogation scenes, the cut cameo featuring Holland's brother Harry as a thief, extra high school scenes, a new post-credits scene, and an updated theatrical poster showcasing all multiverse cast members together.[194][198]

HOME MEDIA & STREAMING

Digital & Disc Release

Released digitally on March 15, 2022, and on 4K Ultra HD Blu-ray, Blu-ray, and DVD on April 12.[202] The home media release featured behind-the-scenes featurettes, a gag reel, and roundtables with both the returning villain actors and the three Spider-Man actors.[205]

Record-Breaking Sales

Set a Vudu pre-release record and sold over 2.1 million digital units ($42 million) in its first week in the US.[206][207] It became the highest-selling physical media title of 2022 in the US according to The NPD Group.[208]

Streaming Rights

Initially streamed exclusively on Starz before moving under Sony's multi-year licensing deals.[209] Following agreements with Disney, Spider-Man: No Way Home became available to stream on Disney+ in the United States on April 15, 2026.[211][213]

BOX OFFICE PERFORMANCE

$610M Estimated Net Profit

Spider-Man: No Way Home grossed $814.9 million in the United States and Canada, and $1.107 billion in other territories for a worldwide total of $1.921 billion.[3] It became the highest-grossing film of 2021,[217] the sixth-highest-grossing film of all time,[218] the third-highest-grossing film in North America,[219] the highest-grossing Spider-Man film, and the highest-grossing film released by Sony.[220] It was the first film to gross over $1 billion since 2019, reaching the milestone third-fastest in cinema history and becoming the first to do so during the COVID-19 pandemic.[221][222] Deadline Hollywood estimated the film's final net profit at $610 million.[223]

In the United States and Canada, No Way Home earned $121.85 million on its opening day (including $50 million from Thursday night previews)—the second-highest opening day of all time behind Avengers: Endgame ($157.4 million).[225] Its domestic opening weekend total of $260 million surpassed Avengers: Infinity War ($257.7 million) for second place overall.[225] The film held the #1 spot at the domestic box office across multiple weekends, eventually crossing $800 million domestically in March 2022 alongside Star Wars: The Force Awakens and Endgame.[207] The theatrical release of The More Fun Stuff Version in September 2022 reclaimed the #1 spot on its first day, generating $6.5 million over the four-day Labor Day weekend.[233][234]

Internationally, No Way Home generated $43.6 million across 15 opening markets, breaking pandemic-era day-one records in South Korea ($5.28 million), the United Kingdom (£7.6 million / $10.1 million), and India (₹320–345 million).[236][237] Over its five-day international opening window, it grossed $340.8 million from 60 markets.[238] As of early 2022, its largest international markets included the United Kingdom ($127.3 million), Mexico ($76.2 million), Australia ($67.9 million), France ($65.2 million), and South Korea ($63.1 million).[240]

PRE-SALE TICKET RECORDS

Advance tickets went on sale at midnight on November 29, 2021, causing major ticketing platforms like Fandango and AMC Theatres to crash under extraordinary user demand.[241] First-day pre-sales on Fandango surpassed Black Widow in just two hours and went on to record the highest 24-hour advance ticket sales since Avengers: Endgame, outperforming Infinity War, Star Wars: The Last Jedi, Far From Home, and Rogue One.[242][243] AMC reported its second-highest single-day ticket sales in history.[243]

Internationally, advance sales in Mexico reached $7 million on day one (40% higher than Endgame), while outselling No Time to Die three-to-one in the UK and beating Endgame by 5% in Brazil.[244] Additional presale records were set in Spain, Poland, Portugal, and Central America.[244]

The review aggregator website Rotten Tomatoes reported an approval rating of 93%, with an average score of 7.9/10, based on 429 reviews. The site's critical consensus reads, "A bigger, bolder Spider-Man sequel, No Way Home expands the franchise's scope and stakes without losing sight of its humor and heart."[245] On CinemaScore, audiences awarded the film a rare "A+" grade—making it the first live-action Spider-Man film and fourth MCU film overall to achieve the rating.[225]
